*** HTML ELEMENTS AND TAGS ***

- Elements are used to create all of the paragraphs, headings, lists, images, and links that make up a webpage.
- Elements are just pieces of content that are wrapped in opening and closing tags.
    - Example of paragraph
        - <p>Hello, world!</p>
    - List of HTML elements: 
        https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/HTML/Reference/Elements

2 reasons for using the correct element tag:
    1. It has a big impact on how they are ranked in search engines.
    2. Impact on accessible they are to users who rely on assistive tech, like screen readers.

Void Elements
    - Some HTML elements do not have a closing tag.
    - They do not wrap content like other tags do.
        - <br> or <img>

- The three parts of an elements tag is opening are the opening tab, the closing tab, and the content wrapped in these tags.

- The <head> element is where we put meta info about our website.
    - No elements inside the <head> should show on the webpage.

- The <body> element is where the content of the page that is displayed will go.
